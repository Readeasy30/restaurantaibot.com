Restaurant Catering Page Checklist

A catering page should help customers understand what you serve, where you serve it, and how to ask for help.

What the page should include

  • Types of events served.
  • Menu options and popular packages.
  • Service area or pickup location.
  • How much notice is needed.
  • Photos of catering trays, setups, or group meals.
  • Phone number, email, or inquiry form.

Good catering page wording

“We offer catering for office lunches, family gatherings, school events, and small celebrations. Call us with your date, guest count, and menu ideas.”

Questions to answer

  • How many people can you serve?
  • Do customers pick up or do you deliver?
  • What are the most popular catering items?
  • Can the restaurant handle special requests?
  • Who should customers contact?

Simple rule

A catering page should make the first call easier. Do not make customers guess what information you need.
