Restaurant Photo Checklist

Good restaurant photos help customers picture the food, the room, and the visit before they arrive.

Must-have photos

  • Front exterior so customers recognize the building.
  • Dining room or counter area so visitors understand the style.
  • Best-selling dishes with clean lighting.
  • Menu board or menu close-up if helpful.
  • Takeout packaging if takeout is important.
  • Team or owner photo if it fits the brand.

Helpful marketing photos

  • Catering trays or party platters.
  • Private event setup.
  • Outdoor seating.
  • Family meal bundle.
  • Seasonal special.
  • Local landmark or neighborhood context near the restaurant.

Photo rules

  • Use real current photos when possible.
  • Keep food clean, bright, and easy to recognize.
  • Remove blurry or outdated photos.
  • Do not make the restaurant look different from the real visit.
  • Add short photo descriptions on website pages when useful.

Simple rule

If the photo would not make a hungry customer more confident, replace it.
